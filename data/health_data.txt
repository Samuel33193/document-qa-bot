📘 1. Introduction to Health
Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease. Maintaining good health involves proper nutrition, regular exercise, mental stability, and preventive healthcare practices.
________________________________________
🍎 2. Importance of Nutrition
Nutrition plays a vital role in maintaining health. A balanced diet includes:
•	Carbohydrates (energy source) 
•	Proteins (body repair and growth) 
•	Fats (energy storage) 
•	Vitamins (body functions regulation) 
•	Minerals (bone strength, immunity) 
Healthy Habits:
•	Drink enough water 
•	Eat fruits and vegetables daily 
•	Avoid junk food

🦠 3. Common Diseases
________________________________________
📄 Diabetes Mellitus
Diabetes is a chronic condition where blood sugar levels are too high.
Types:
•	Type 1 Diabetes 
•	Type 2 Diabetes 
•	Gestational Diabetes 
Symptoms:
•	Frequent urination 
•	Increased thirst 
•	Fatigue 
•	Blurred vision 
Causes:
•	Genetics 
•	Obesity 
•	Lack of physical activity 
Prevention:
•	Healthy diet 
•	Regular exercise 
•	Maintain body weight 
Treatment:
•	Insulin therapy 
•	Oral medications 
•	Lifestyle changes 
________________________________________
📄 Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
Hypertension is a condition where blood pressure is consistently high.
Symptoms:
•	Headaches 
•	Dizziness 
•	Often no symptoms 
Causes:
•	High salt intake 
•	Stress 
•	Obesity 
Prevention:
•	Reduce salt 
•	Exercise regularly 
•	Manage stress 
Treatment:
•	Blood pressure medications 
•	Lifestyle changes 
________________________________________
📄 Heart Disease
Heart disease includes conditions affecting the heart.
Symptoms:
•	Chest pain 
•	Shortness of breath 
•	Fatigue 
Risk Factors:
•	Smoking 
•	High cholesterol 
•	Diabetes 
Prevention:
•	Healthy lifestyle 
•	Regular exercise 
Treatment:
•	Medications 
•	Surgery 
________________________________________
📄 COVID-19
COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by a virus.
Symptoms:
•	Fever 
•	Cough 
•	Loss of smell 
Transmission:
•	Air droplets 
•	Close contact 
Prevention:
•	Vaccination 
•	Masks 
•	Hand hygiene 
Treatment:
•	Supportive care 
•	Hospitalization in severe cases 
________________________________________
📄 Anemia
Anemia is a condition with low red blood cells.
Symptoms:
•	Weakness 
•	Pale skin 
•	Fatigue 
Causes:
•	Iron deficiency 
•	Vitamin deficiency 
Treatment:
•	Iron supplements 
•	Diet changes 
________________________________________
